what defines STEM Assiut.

The most defining aspect of Assiut STEM School is its continuous seeking to establish a new mentality in the underprivileged areas of Upper Egypt. The Totally newborn mindset is what is unique here. Once a student, or even a teacher, joins this amazing community, they become a different person. By being the first ever boarding school in Upper Egypt, Assiut STEM School opened up new doors of independency to the Upper Egyptian girls, where girls are finally allowed to explore communities beyond their own families, activities other than curricular studies, and new aspects of science that could finally define a vibrant character of the Upper Egyptian identity. In addition, Assiut STEM School introduces a mindset of learning that was never before applied in the Upper Egyptian cities in which scientific research is applied to derive the concepts of study, then those concepts are applied in a capstone project that addresses some of Egypt's grand challenge.

Capstone and student research

Student research is an integral part of the curriculum of Assiut STEM School with students required to complete a Capstone project each semester. In groups, students work to devise a project that addresses one of Egyptâ€™ s grand challenges. The grading of capstone is based on five factors: Scientific portfolio, poster, journals, prototype and a capstone exhibition. A panel of university professors give their assessment on the project. Main Capstone Topics include: Urban congestion & construction, Industry, Energy, Water, and Communication.
Capstone projects give students the chance to apply the concepts they learn from various STEM subjects in real life applications. By working on their research projects, students learn about scientific methodology and scientific writing. Under the supervision of the capstone leader, Students develop their projects at various agencies, including corporate, university, and government institutions.
