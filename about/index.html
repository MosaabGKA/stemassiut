Overview

Ranked the best Egyptian secondary school in Upper Egypt, Assiut STEM School is the product of a partnership between the U.S. Agency for International Development (USAID) and its sub-recipients the ECASE project to provide technical assistance to the Ministry of Education to establish a model STEM school in each Egyptian governorate. Assiut STEM school was founded in 2015 and is a two semester based school that runs from September to June (Changed to October to July due to COVID-19).
Accreditation: The school is accredited by the United States Agency for International Development (USAID) and the Egyptian Ministry of Education.

Vision & Mission

With the aim to nurture Upper Egyptian future shapers who demonstrate entrepreneurial leadership, high ethical standards, and civic engagement – regardless of their socioeconomic background- Assiut STEM School offers a rigorous program that focuses on scientific, mathematical, and technological fields with the first introduction to project-based learning in Upper Egypt which ensures students’ life interaction with the subjects they study. We believe that the school is designed to create “centers of excellence” that embody trans- disciplinary collaboration, real world applications, critical thinking, and a pioneering spirit to support the new Egyptian vision as a country equipped with both the human capital and the institutional resources to compete and excel in the international marketplace.

Boarding School Experience

Education takes on a broad meaning at Assiut STEM High school. As a Boarding school, we recognize that we have an explicit mission not only to educate students in the classroom, but also help them become well-rounded individuals. In our boarding school, we bring a kind of family feel to education and make school more of an intimate place. Within the dorms, students develop their own life skills and natural abilities. They are to make choices that matter, balance their time and develop their social skills by frequent interactions with their peers. With such circumstances, the school creates a strong network of students, faculty, and staff and a feeling of warmth and welcoming within the community.

Admission

Prospective students must meet one of the following requirements to apply

  • Total of 9th grade: not less than 98%
  • 100% on one out of three subjects of Mathematics, English, and Science
  • Total of 9th grade: not less than 95%
  • 100% on two out of three subjects of Mathematics, English, and Science
  • Total of 9th grade higher than 90%
  • With a research patent

The competitive admission process ensures the academic capabilities of the enrolling students. Applicants are tested in 4 areas (English, IQ, Mathematics, and Science) and then ranked nationally. The top 2000 (50% boys and 50% girls) students are granted admission and distributed mostly to their nearest STEM school geographically. The admission process is very competitive. this year 18000 students applied and 2100 students were admitted with a total acceptance rate of 11.6%.

Activities

Once a week, students attend an activities session. This session affords students the ability to seek out enrichment while also providing them the opportunity to participate in activities pertaining , but not limited to, Library, Physical education, Music, and Art. Additionally, there are after school activities, which are led by the students in cooperation with the school. Examples of these activities are Football, Table tennis, athletics, theatre, handwork.

STEM Labs & Fab Lab

In addition to Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geology, Mechanics, and Computer labs, Assiut STEM School has a fabrication lab. Fab lab STEM is an environment that promotes innovation within students by providing collaborative open makerspace equipped with digital fabrication, prototyping machines, hands-on workshops and an MIT curriculum based training. With such facilities, the Fab lab welcomes students to use the digital fabrication machines at Assiut STEM Fab Lab in order to support promising ideas and prototypes related to subject areas and capstone projects. With a goal of empowering the students in different sectors, students are encouraged to participate in designing and making the products they use such as physical objects and machines to materialize their ideas.
